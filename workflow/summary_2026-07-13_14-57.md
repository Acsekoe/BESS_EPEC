## Current Objective

## Diagnosis: The Old VOLL Profit Was Optimistic Dual Selection

A diagnostic ("degeneracy probe") solved the standalone LLP at the MPEC's fixed capacities, then maximized/minimized investor spot revenue over the set of *optimal dual solutions* (dual feasibility + dual objective within 1 EUR of the LLP optimum):

- Old (single VOLL tier) data: MPEC-reported revenue `3,989,898 EUR/day` matched the **maximum** over optimal duals to the cent; the minimum was `2,984,368`; HiGHS basic duals gave `3,434,990`. Primal dispatch was essentially unique (throughput range 0.4 MWh) — the ambiguity was purely in the duals.
- Mechanism, visible in hours 19-21: only two prices were pinned (peaker marginal at N5, shed marginal at N4), leaving one free direction in dual space. The LLP vertex had `mu_L12 = 0` (L12 degenerately binding, lambda_N2 = 5,508, lambda_N3 = 6,706); the MPEC selected `mu_L12 = -8,194.78`, lifting lambda_N2 = lambda_N3 = 10,000 exactly where its BESS discharged, stopping where dual feasibility caps lambda at VOLL.
- Line duals exceeding VOLL (e.g. `17,926`) are correct: nodal spreads form via fractional PTDF differences, so mu must exceed the spread. This is why `--dual-bound-scale` needs >= 2.

Conclusion: the formulation is mathematically sound; the problem was dual (price) degeneracy created by VOLL scarcity, plus a miscalibrated benchmark in which even pessimistic pricing yields ~3M EUR/day.

## Implemented This Session: Tiered Price-Elastic Demand

Stepwise demand bid curve replacing the single all-or-nothing VOLL shed. Each tier k can curtail up to `share_k * demand[n,t]` at willingness-to-pay `wtp_k`. Default schedule (defined in `prepare_data.py`, `DEFAULT_DEMAND_TIERS`):

| Tier | Share | WTP (EUR/MWh) |
|---|---:|---:|
| T1_300 | 5% | 300 |
| T2_1000 | 5% | 1,000 |
| T3_3000 | 10% | 3,000 |
| T_VOLL | 80% (auto top-up) | 10,000 |

Changed files:

- `model/prepare_data.py`: emits `demand_tiers` into `market_data.json`. Optional Excel sheet `demand_tiers` (columns: `tier`, `share`, `wtp_eur_per_mwh`) overrides the default; shares must not exceed 1; remainder topped up at VOLL. Workbook reading now accepts extra sheets.
- `model/primal_market_clearing_model.py`: `MarketData` gained `demand_tiers`, `tier_share`, `tier_wtp` (older JSONs without tiers fall back to one 100% VOLL tier, so old behavior is reproducible). `P_shed` is now indexed `[k,n,t]`, objective uses tier WTPs, bound is `share_k * demand`.
- `model/dual_market_clearing_model.py`: `xi_shed[k,n,t]`, stationarity `lam + xi_k <= wtp_k`, dual objective demand term `D * (lam + sum_k share_k * xi_k)`.
