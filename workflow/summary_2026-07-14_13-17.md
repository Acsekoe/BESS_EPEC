## Current Objective

Eliminate lower-level dual-price degeneracy structurally (not just diagnostically) before the multi-investor EPEC rebuild, by replacing the tiered LP demand with a strictly concave demand curve so lower-level prices are unique.

## DECISION: Strictly Convex Quadratic Demand (implemented this session)

The 07-13/07-14 diagnostics showed the degeneracy had become the result itself: for the 50 MW existing-fleet case the same investment was worth 9,552 (MPEC optimistic) / 6,157 (min-norm) / 861 (HiGHS reference) / ~-960 (pessimistic) EUR/day - the profit sign depended on dual selection. Post-solve settlement rules cannot fix the optimizer's incentive, so the structural fix was implemented.

### Formulation

- The tiered curtailment `P_shed[k,n,t]` is replaced by one shed variable `P_shed[n,t] in [0, D_{n,t}]` with strictly convex cost `alpha*s + beta*s^2/(2*D)`, i.e. marginal WTP `alpha + beta*(s/D)` - a strictly decreasing inverse demand curve in the curtailed share, over the WHOLE demand range (no VOLL cliff).
- Curve estimated from the elastic demand tiers in `market_data_experiment.json` (`fit_quadratic_demand_curve`): tiers below VOLL are observations of marginal WTP at their cumulative-share midpoints (ascending WTP = curtailment merit order); the 90% VOLL tier is excluded as inelastic remainder. Unconstrained LS gives a negative intercept, so the fit re-anchors through the origin. **Result: marginal WTP = 0 + 17,631.58 x curtailed_share EUR/MWh** (crosses VOLL at ~57% curtailment).
- MPEC embedding: shed stationarity becomes `lam + xi <= (beta/D)*s` (primal-dependent marginal cost, still linear); strong duality uses the **Wolfe dual** - the dual objective gains `-sum 0.5*(beta/D)*s^2`. With stationarity written on the same primal variables, zero gap still enforces every individual complementarity condition, so the reformulation stays exact.
- Why this kills the mu-inflation artifact: with an elastic margin at every node-hour, `lam` is pinned to the demand curve at the (unique) cleared curtailment; binding-line duals `mu` are backed out of price differences instead of being free levers. Prices can never exceed any consumer's marginal WTP (no more implied scarcity > VOLL).

Interpretation:

1. **Belief = settlement.** The MPEC can no longer pick favorable prices; the optimistic-price artifact is structurally gone. The settlement machinery becomes a validation layer, not a correction.
2. **The 1.04M -> 342k baseline drop IS the removed artifact**: strategic withholding to the VOLL cliff. Prices now ride the demand curve (peak ~2,039 = edge of the fitted elastic band).
3. **50 MW investment pattern changed**: concentrates at N1 (41.1 MW, 5.6 h - trapped-wind congestion arbitrage) + N2 (14.1 MW); N3-N5 ~0. Profit sign can no longer flip with dual selection.
4. **Residual (known, mild): dispatch-side degeneracy.** 50 MW case: reference-QP dispatch profit 11,929 vs MPEC 12,516 (~588 EUR/day) because two identical-efficiency fleets are interchangeable in dispatch. MPEC's own dispatch at the unique reference prices earns 12,515.6 ~= 12,516.3, proving prices are unique. One thesis sentence, not a fix.
