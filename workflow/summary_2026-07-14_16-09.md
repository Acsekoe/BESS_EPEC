## Current objective

Understand why the single-investor MPEC produces absurd nodal prices (tens/hundreds of thousands EUR/MWh) when demand response is disabled, decide the thesis-quality price-formation design, and settle the demand-response / willingness-to-pay (WTP) curve question.

## Diagnosis (confirmed from code, no changes made)

The MPEC embeds the lower level via **primal feasibility + dual feasibility + strong duality** (`model/single_investor_mpec.py`, `m.strong_duality`). This is exact, but it contains the **entire dual optimal face** of the clearing LP. Since investor revenue uses the embedded dual directly (`m.spot_revenue_expr = sum(lam * (P_dis - P_ch))`), Ipopt performs **optimistic dual selection**: it picks the price vector on the optimal face that maximizes investor profit. The reported prices are genuinely optimal LP duals — just strategically selected ones.

Key mechanisms:

- **Fixed demand removes the demand-side stationarity condition.** With `P_shed` fixed to 0, no constraint pins `lam` from above. In hours where every supply resource is at a bound (or degenerate ties exist), the dual face is unbounded upward; `lam` rides to the artificial box bound `dual_bound_scale * DEFAULT_FIXED_DEMAND_DUAL_BOUND_EUR_PER_MWH = 10 * 30,000 = 300,000` EUR/MWh. That bound is exactly where the observed "hundreds of thousands" comes from.
- **The investor can manufacture degeneracy.** `X_power`/`X_energy` sit on lower-level RHS and in the dual objective. Canonical exploit: in an hour with `P_discharge = X_power`, `sig_dis < 0` is allowed, discharge stationarity `lam + sig_dis + gam/eta <= 0` no longer caps `lam`; raising `lam` by Δ and lowering `sig_dis` by Δ keeps strong duality satisfied (`D*Δ` offset by `X_power*(-Δ)`). The price becomes an indeterminate scarcity rent.
- **Storage DC-OPF is systematically degenerate** (generator exactly at capacity while marginal; storage power and SOC bounds binding simultaneously; SOC periodicity ties), so this is structural, not a data accident.
- **Residual dual selection exists even in quadratic mode**: current `summary.json` shows `mpec_lambda_max_abs_diff_vs_reference = 29.87 EUR/MWh` and `optimistic_minus_reference_profit ≈ 15,895 EUR/day` (~3% of profit) — from node-hours with zero shedding where the curve is not marginal.

## Why the old model "worked"

`diplomarbeit/bess_epec_model.py` bounded `lambda_spot in [0, 3000]` **and** used relaxed KKT complementarity (`COMPL_RELAX = 10`). In a strong-duality formulation, a dual box that excludes the true dual optimal face makes `strong_duality` infeasible (weak duality argument); the old model avoided that only because relaxed complementarity tolerates lower-level suboptimality. Two hidden distortions cancelling — not acceptable for the thesis. A bare cap also truncates rather than removes multiplicity, and the `>= 0` bound forbids legitimate negative prices.

## Decided design: elastic demand / WTP curve as market design (not a helper)

The mathematically clean fix is to make lower-level prices **unique by construction on the demand side**. Keep the quadratic curtailment curve and reframe it in the thesis as *elastic demand with finite willingness-to-pay / scarcity pricing*:

- Curve: shedding `s` at nodal demand `D` costs `alpha*s + beta*s^2/(2D)`, so marginal WTP = `alpha + beta*(s/D)` — a strictly decreasing inverse demand curve in the curtailed share.
- **Where shedding is interior**, complementarity forces `lam = alpha + (beta/D)*shed` — price pinned exactly, no dual face (strictly convex QP term).
- **Where shedding is zero**, `xi_shed = 0` gives `lam <= alpha` — the curve always supplies an upper price anchor. A price cap done correctly *is* load shedding at finite WTP: `lam <= cap` emerges as dual feasibility of a real primal variable, strong duality stays exact.
- **Calibration to decide tomorrow:** set `alpha` to a defensible market price cap or VOLL (candidates: EU harmonized day-ahead cap ~4,000 EUR/MWh; VOLL estimates ~10,000–15,000 EUR/MWh; current default is `alpha = 500`, `beta ≈ 17,632`). Make the curve steep enough that equilibrium curtailment is negligible, and report shed volumes (~0) to show the investor cannot game shedding beyond the cap — that is what a cap is for.
- **Optional complement:** small strictly convex generation-cost terms (`eps * P_gen^2`, with Wolfe-dual correction as already done for the shed term) pin prices in non-scarcity hours where a generator is interior. Helpful for the residual ~30 EUR/MWh gap, but NOT a substitute for the demand curve — it cannot pin scarcity hours where nothing is interior.

Settlement: use the **embedded `lam`** (valid once duals are unique), keep the reference-QP settlement as the reported consistency check (`optimistic_minus_reference_profit → ~0` is the evidence dual selection is closed). Do NOT settle at standalone reference prices inside the MPEC (investor would optimize against prices it isn't paid — ill-posed, and poisons the EPEC diagonalization).

Thesis narrative: the fixed-demand price explosion is a genuine methodological finding, not a bug — with perfectly inelastic demand the bilevel problem is ill-posed (optimistic dual selection), and elastic demand restores well-defined price formation. This also explains the predecessor model's `[0, 3000]` bound. Standard references for demand bids with finite utility in strategic MPECs: Ruiz & Conejo (2009); Gabriel/Conejo/Fuller/Hobbs/Ruiz complementarity book.

EPEC stage: every investor's MPEC must use the same lower-level demand curve so all investors face identical price formation during diagonalization; otherwise each best-responds to its own favorable dual selection and the fixed point is meaningless.
