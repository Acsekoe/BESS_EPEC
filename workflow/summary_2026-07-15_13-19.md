Christoph (supervisor) asked for the 5-bus network to be revised so that spot-market price signals are more meaningful — the current calibration was suspected of showing heavy congestion in every high-demand hour. ## Diagnosis (confirmed from existing EPEC output, no reruns needed)

Data source: `model/output/epec/jacobi/investor_I1/line_hour_flows_duals.csv` and `model/output/epec/jacobi/joint_node_hour_prices.csv` (an existing solved run), cross-checked against `model/data/input/bess_epec_inputs.xlsx` and `model/data/processed/market_data.json`.

### 1. Peak-hour congestion is real and severe

- Morning peak (hours 7–9): line `L12` (N1→N2, limit 400 MW) and `L45` (N4→N5, limit 240 MW) sit at **100% utilization**. Nodal price at N2 spikes to **~€4,000/MWh** while N1/N5 stay at €40–80/MWh.
- Evening peak (hours 19–21): `L45` binds again; N2 reaches **~€2,225/MWh**, N1 **~€993/MWh**.
- All other hours: max line utilization is 51–98%, non-binding, and price is flat (€40 or €70–80) across all nodes — i.e. outside the two peak windows there is no locational price signal at all, just a flat system price.

### 2. Midday prices don't drop either — a second, independent structural cause

At hour 14 (PV at its daily max: 430/430 MW at N3, wind 10/10 MW at N1), `G_Base` (600 MW cap, €40/MWh) is **also dispatched to its full 600 MW cap** even though transmission lines are only ~52% utilized (not the bottleneck). Root cause: total conventional capacity is calibrated with essentially **zero systemwide margin over peak demand**:

| Resource | Capacity |
|---|---:|
| `G_Base` (N1, €40/MWh) | 600 MW |
| `G_Peak` (N5, €80/MWh) | 600 MW |
| **Total dispatchable** | **1200 MW** |
| System peak demand | **1200 MW** |

Because base+peak capacity exactly equals peak demand, there is no slack anywhere in the day. Even at midday, once BESS charging (or any incremental load) is added on top of demand, the system runs out of €40 headroom and price is pulled toward the €80 peaker — regardless of how much PV is available locally at N3. PV is already maxed out (430 MW peak) but has no room to actually undercut anything, because the swing producer (`G_Base`) is already saturated.

### Two independent structural causes → two independent fixes needed

1. **Line limits vs. topology**: 90% of load sits at central nodes N2/N3/N4 while cheap supply (base generator + wind) sits at peripheral N1. `L12` (400 MW) and `L45` (240 MW) are tight enough that this saturates at every demand peak, producing the €2,000–4,000/MWh spikes. → *Loosen transmission limits.*
2. **Zero generation capacity margin**: `G_Base` + `G_Peak` = 1200 MW = system peak load exactly. PV alone cannot create a real price trough while the cheapest conventional unit is already capacity-bound. → *Add generation headroom, not just more PV.*

Ramping up PV without addressing (2) will likely leave the midday trough shallow, since `G_Base` would still saturate near the trough.

## Decision: defer implementation

Alex explicitly asked not to implement any of this yet — this is step one (diagnosis + agenda slide) and the actual parameter changes (line limit values, generator capacity margin, PV scaling factor) are a second step, to be decided together. Two open scoping questions were raised and left unanswered on purpose:

- Whether to add generation capacity margin alongside loosening lines and scaling PV (recommended, since PV alone won't fix the midday flatness — see cause #2 above).
- How aggressive the line-limit loosening should be (moderate ~1.5x, aggressive ~2x, or minimal) — noting that *some* congestion at extreme peaks is probably still desirable for the strategic-siting narrative; the goal is to remove guaranteed saturation, not eliminate congestion entirely.
