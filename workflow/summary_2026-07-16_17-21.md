# Handoff Summary — Optimistic Dual Selection: Diagnosis, Failed Fixes, Decision to Report

Date: 2026-07-16 17:21

## Current objective

Understand and deal with the belief-vs-settlement profit gap in the spot-market BESS
EPEC. Along the way, add a nodal-vs-system (zonal) settlement price option. Outcome:
the gap is a **structural** feature of the optimistic MPEC, so we **report it** (neutral
settlement price + gap diagnostic) rather than trying to engineer it away. All

system-price toggle.

## Key decision

**Accept optimistic dual selection and report it.** Reported prices/profits use the
neutral joint re-clearing (the market-consistent LMP); the belief-vs-settled gap and the
max MPEC-vs-neutral lambda difference are reported as diagnostics in every result table.
No primal regularization and no price penalty are used.

## What the gap actually is (mechanism)

- Inside each investor's MPEC the price `lam[n,t]` (or `lam_sys[t]`) is a **decision
  variable**, not data. The lower level is embedded via a strong-duality equality
  (`primal_objective_expr == dual_objective_expr`, [single_investor_mpec.py:571]), so the
  solver may pick **any** optimal `(dispatch, lambda)` pair — and, maximizing investor
  profit, it selects the most favorable price from the degenerate optimal dual set.
- The **reported** prices come from a separate neutral re-clearing after convergence
  (`compute_joint_settlement` in [epec_results.py]); these are the textbook LMPs (dual of
  nodal balance) and are what every participant actually settles at.
- So there are two distinct lambdas: (A) MPEC belief (self-selected, drives investment,
  shown by `--print-mpec-lambdas`), (B) neutral settlement (reported, in
  `joint_node_hour_prices.csv`). `belief_minus_settled` and
  `mpec_lambda_max_abs_diff_vs_joint` quantify (A)−(B).

### Baseline nodal 9-bus Seidel (seidel_9bus_seidel), the reference for all comparisons
- Converged 17 iters; joint lambda 0–75; total 211.5 MW.
- I1 (8%): 126.6 MW, settled 9,089, gap +4,021, lam_diff 11.36.
- I2 (12%): 84.9 MW, settled 4,887, gap +2,215, lam_diff 11.36.
- Decomposition (nodal): the gap is **100% price tilt** — believed dispatch = settled
  dispatch to the MWh; 0 dispatch effect, 0 degradation effect. The MPEC tilts both legs:
  believes it charges the congested PV node (N8, h13/h15) for **free** (0 vs 11.36) and
  discharges richer (h7: 75 vs 65). 80 of 216 node-hours diverge.

## System-price (zonal) settlement — new feature, kept

Toggle `SYSTEM_PRICE_SETTLEMENT` / `--settlement-price {nodal,system}`. Investors both
optimize and settle at the uniform per-hour system price `lam_sys[t]` (dual of the system
balance) instead of the nodal LMP. Implemented consistently in belief (MPEC objective) and
settlement so it actually changes siting.

Run seidel_9bus_seidel_system (converged 27 iters): investment **spread** (N3, N6, N7, N8;
total ~326 MW vs 211 nodal) and total capacity rose, because uniform pricing strips the
congestion rent and lifts the price floor (32.5–87.2 vs 0–75). Gaps were **larger**
(~6,656 / 6,673) even though per-unit lam_diff was small (8.93): uniform pricing gives the
optimistic selection more leverage over aggregate volume, plus a degradation illusion
(believed throughput < settled). This system-price run output is retained.

## Fixes tried and rejected (the main learning)

All three aimed to shrink the gap; all failed for instructive reasons.

1. **Generation Tikhonov `0.5*eps*P_gen^2`** (eps=0.001): backfired. It is not a
   tie-breaker — it tilts the merit-order supply curve, so arbitrage value rises,
   capacities ballooned 211→337 MW, and the gap **grew** (4,021→10,020). The degenerate
   node-hours (curtailed PV, congestion) are not set by a marginal generator, so a
   generation term cannot pin them. Rejected.

2. **Storage-dispatch Tikhonov `0.5*eps*(P_ch^2+P_dis^2)`** (eps=0.001): well-behaved
   (lambda range preserved 0–75, capacities ~stable) but did **not** shrink the gap
   (lam_diff 11.4→18.9). Root cause: at eps=0.001 the added marginal cost is ~0.1 EUR/MWh
   at 100 MW — two orders of magnitude too small to pin an ~11 EUR/MWh dual gap. To pin it
   you need eps ~ 0.1 EUR/MW^2, i.e. an ~11 EUR/MWh cycling cost, which is economically
   significant and changes the equilibrium. **Fundamental tension for any primal
   regularization: big enough to pin the dual = big enough to distort the model.** Rejected.

3. **Soft belief-price penalty `-c_pen * sum (lam_belief - pi_ref)^2`** with `pi_ref` the
   previous pass's neutral price (c_pen=100): **did not converge** (oscillated to the
   60-iter cap). It disciplined the price (lam_diff 11.36→1.5) but the investor paid the
   penalty down through **capacity** — capacities ballooned 211→327 MW and settled profit
   collapsed (I1 −58%, I2 −81%). Because lambda and capacity are coupled, any penalty on
   lambda leaks into the investment decision; the lagged anchor also drives oscillation.
   Rejected.

**Conclusion:** the belief–settlement gap is structural to an optimistic MPEC with a
degenerate lower-level dual. The clean way to eliminate it would be to change the
equilibrium concept to price-taking (drop lambda from the objective and pay at the neutral
reference — the "hard replacement"), which was discussed but not implemented; it is a
modeling decision for the supervisor, not a numerical fix.
