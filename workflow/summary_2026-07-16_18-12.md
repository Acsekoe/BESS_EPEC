# Handoff Summary — 4-Investor EPEC with Portfolio-Backed (RES-Owning) Investors

Date: 2026-07-16 18:12

## Current objective

Extend the 2-investor spot-market EPEC to **four heterogeneous investors** so the
model shows "real economics": two stand-alone merchant BESS investors plus two
**portfolio-backed** investors that own a share of the *existing* wind/PV fleet and
earn its inframarginal spot rent alongside BESS arbitrage. The two portfolios are
the same WACC (8%) and differ only by a **wind-vs-solar ownership tilt**.

## Key design decision

Portfolio ownership is expressed as **fractional rent shares per existing
generator** (`s_{i,g} in [0,1]`), not whole-generator assignment, so a single wind
unit and two PV units can be split into a "much wind / little solar" vs "much solar
/ little wind" contrast with no double-counting. Shares sum to 1.0 per generator
across the two portfolios. This is an **upper-level objective-only** change: `P_gen`
and `lambda` already live in every investor's MPEC, so the lower-level clearing and
the rival-aggregation approximation are untouched (still exact for the shared eta).

## Investor set (`--investor-set portfolio4`, 9-bus)

| Investor | WACC | Type | Ownership shares |
|---|---:|---|---|
| I1 | 8% | stand-alone merchant BESS | — |
| I2 | 12% | stand-alone merchant BESS | — |
| I3 | 8% | wind-heavy RES portfolio + BESS | Wind_N1 0.8, PV_N6 0.2, PV_N8 0.2 |
| I4 | 8% | solar-heavy RES portfolio + BESS | Wind_N1 0.2, PV_N6 0.8, PV_N8 0.8 |

Generation rent (credited at the settlement price, `MC=0` for RES):
`GenRent_i = sum_g s_{i,g} * sum_t (pi_{n(g),t} - C_g) * P_{g,t}`.

Seidel, damping 0.7, nodal settlement, joint lambda 30.28–75.00 EUR/MWh.

|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| I1 (merchant 8%) | 123.0 | 382 | 12,414 | 0 | 2,557 | **918** |

All figures EUR/day. Optimistic-minus-settled gaps: I1 +5,695, I2 +4,577, I3

their generation nodes — consistent with the reported optimistic-dual methodology).

### Economics read (this is the "real economics" the extension targets)
- **Wind out-earns solar** on generation rent (378k vs 292k) despite identical WACC:
  wind (N1) produces into higher-price night/shoulder hours, while the dominant PV
  (N8) crashes its own midday nodal price toward the floor, so solar rent per MWh is
  lower. The tilt alone differentiates two otherwise-identical 8% investors.
- **Merchant arbitrage is competed thin** (I1 918, I2 126 EUR/day) — with four
  players contesting the same nodal access, spot margins nearly wash against CAPEX,
  while the portfolio investors' value is dominated by generation rent.
- **Siting**: both portfolio investors place BESS at N8 (the midday-cheap dominant-PV
  node, ~99.7 MW total there); I1 sits mostly at N1 (charge cheap wind at night) and
  N7; N7/N8 are the contested nodes. No projection events (nodal limit never
  over-subscribed after damping).

## Caveat — did NOT formally converge

Hit the 60-iteration cap (`state.converged = False`, so the process exits 1 — this
is the expected non-convergence return code, not a crash). **Power capacities
converged** (`max_rel dP ~ 0.008`); **energy capacities oscillate** (`dE ~ 0.07–0.29`,
above tol 0.01). The oscillation is driven by economically-negligible near-zero
nodal positions flickering in/out (relative delta amplified by the small `floor_mwh
= 2.0` denominator), not by the real allocation, which is stable from ~iter 14 (I3/I4
economics fixed to the euro). It is a convergence-metric artefact, not a modelling
error.
