## Corrected convergence diagnosis (supersedes the 18-12 caveat)

Earlier the non-convergence was attributed to floor-noise from near-zero nodes.
**That was wrong.** Digging into the trajectory:

- **Power converges fine** (`max_rel dP` ~0.007-0.017 in the tail, under 2%).
- **Energy does not**: `dE` parks at 0.07-0.29. The binding driver is a **genuine
  limit cycle at node N8** (the only node at its 100 MW limit): I4 swings its N8
  energy ~46<->65 MWh and I2 ~42<->59 MWh, iteration after iteration (10-19 MWh
  real swings, not sub-MWh flips).
- Root cause is the **proportional-projection** rationing of the shared node limit:
  when N8 oversubscribes, all holders are scaled equally — an ad-hoc, non-economic
  step that lets the two portfolio/merchant investors overshoot and swap N8 energy
  forever. Damping 0.7 is too weak to kill it.
- **Right lever = more damping**, not a bigger floor. Recommended run (not yet
  executed to completion by the user):
  `... --investor-set portfolio4 --update-rule seidel --tag portfolio4 --damping 0.5`
  Watch the tail `dE` decay below 0.02. If a couple of truly negligible near-zero
  nodes (e.g. I1/N6, old 0.04 MWh) still poke above 2%, add `--floor-mwh 10`.

### On the `0.7` plateaus in the log
`x_new - x_old = a*(x_proposed - x_old)`, so `rel_delta = a*|x_proposed-x_old|/max(|x_old|,floor)`.
When the largest-moving node's best response is to **exit** (`x_proposed ~ 0`) and it
is above the floor, `rel_delta = a = 0.7` exactly — a node being abandoned, decaying
x0.3 per iteration. The big periodic spikes (iter 8 dP=42, etc.) are the mirror image:
a near-floor node suddenly **entered** with large capacity.

## Nodal-access auction — design discussion and conclusions (NOT implemented)

Motivation: the shared node limit is currently rationed by proportional projection,
which (a) hides willingness-to-pay for scarce access and (b) causes the N8 limit
cycle. Pricing access would reveal who values each node and likely converge cleaner.
This matches the long-pending "nodal access auction / `kappa[n]`" item.

### Two mechanisms considered
1. **Uniform clearing price `kappa[n]`** (shadow price / Walrasian tatonnement):
   add `kappa[n]*X_power[i,n]` as a cost in each investor's MPEC; update
   `kappa[n] <- max(0, kappa[n] + step*(sum_i X_power[i,n] - Limit_n))` in an outer
   loop that **replaces** the projection step. `kappa[n]` is the dual of the node
   limit: positive only on binding nodes, zero elsewhere by complementarity.
   Continuous, tractable, standard variational-equilibrium selection.
2. **Strategic access bid** (user's idea): the per-MW bid is itself a decision
   variable, merit-order allocates the scarce MW to highest bidders.

### Conclusions
- **They are not equivalent.** Uniform-price with truthful bids = the shadow price
  (mechanism 1). Making the bid strategic adds **buy-side market power / bid-shading**
  on top of the existing spot-market power. Strategic bid = shadow price + demand
  withholding on access. The gap between them isolates access-market power.
- **Unit is EUR/MW**, not EUR/MWh — the scarce grid resource is the *connection*
  (power), not energy.
- **Total installed BESS should NOT drop much under mechanism 1 in the current
  calibration** (this corrects an earlier over-claim). Reason: **only N8 actually
  binds** (99.7/100); N7 is close (85) but slack; all other nodes have large slack.
  So `kappa[n]=0` everywhere except N8. The charge only touches N8's 100 MW, which is
  capped regardless — so the auction **reallocates + prices** N8, it does not shrink
  total capacity. At most a small dip if a displaced marginal N8 holder (e.g. I2's
  19.7 MW) finds no profitable node to redeploy to.
- The merchants' razor-thin profit (918 / 126 EUR/day) comes from **spot-market
  competition, not free access**, so pricing access would not remove that capacity.
- Therefore, in the current setup the auction's value is **"reveal who wins the one
  genuinely scarce node (N8) and at what price"** — it quantifies the wind-vs-solar
  portfolio fight over N8 — rather than curbing overinvestment.
- **Lever if a broader scarcity story is wanted:** tighten `Limit_node`
  (e.g. 50 MW/node). Then several nodes bind, `kappa>0` at more of them, and total
  BESS would fall meaningfully. Open design choice: access market as a
  *pricing/allocation* story on one node (keep 100 MW) vs a *scarcity* story across
  many nodes (lower the limit).
- **Tractability warning:** the strategic merit-order variant embeds a discrete
  in-the-money/out-of-money selection inside each investor's already-MPEC problem
  (MPCC / possible binaries) and conflates access scarcity with strategic
  under-bidding. **Recommended sequencing:** implement mechanism 1 (uniform
  `kappa[n]`) as the competitive benchmark first, then optionally add strategic bids
  as an extension and compare.

### Open decision before any auction coding
Is the access payment a **real cost** (enters NPV, reduces profit, and — only if many
nodes bind — reduces total BESS) or a **pure rationing signal** (nets out)? This
determines the objective term and whether total investment changes.
