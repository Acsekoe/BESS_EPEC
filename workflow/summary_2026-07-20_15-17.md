## Interpretation of the current EPEC issue

Do not equate the large relative capacity metric with immediate economic
failure. Stable profits can coexist with capacity movement when the objective
is flat and storage relocates among nearly price-equivalent nodes. The relative
metric can also exaggerate movement of small nodal capacities because its
denominator floors are 1 MW and 2 MWh.

Nevertheless, the run is numerically expensive:

- each failed best response is retried once at a 0.9-shrunk previous capacity;
- with `--max-cpu-time 120`, one fully failed investor can consume roughly
  240 seconds before being marked failed;
- high PV, an exactly binding L78, and competition for the 100 MW N8 connection
  create sharp active-set changes and a flat/degenerate siting landscape;
- portfolio investors add generator-rent terms to the nonconvex MPEC objective;
- the default dual bound is `10 * VOLL = 100,000 EUR/MWh`, far above the current
  approximately 101 EUR/MWh price range and therefore potentially poorly scaled.

The current run should be allowed to continue unless failures repeat or both
aggregate and nodal capacities clearly fail to settle. Profit stability alone
is encouraging but is not the EPEC convergence condition; equilibrium requires
a best-response fixed point in strategies.
