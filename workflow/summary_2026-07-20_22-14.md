# Handoff Summary — IEEE-9 Congestion EPEC (Seidel)

Date: 2026-07-20 22:14

Ran the four-investor EPEC on
`market_data_IEEE_9Bus_congestion.json` with Gauss–Seidel updates, a 100 MW
nodal limit, damping 0.7, and 2% convergence tolerance. The run converged in
7 iterations; every investor solve was optimal, no projection was needed, and
the largest final undamped power change was 0.076 MW.

The EPEC installed 239.004 MW / 827.044 MWh, essentially matching the saved
central-planner result of 238.924 MW / 826.862 MWh. The small aggregate gap is
mostly caused by positive EPEC capacity floors at inactive nodes. Both place
100 MW at N8; relative to the planner, the EPEC shifts about 2.21 MW from N9
to N3. Revalued at the planner's 8% WACC, approximate EPEC social cost is
440,068 EUR/day versus 439,481 EUR/day for the planner (+0.134%).

Midday congestion remains clear. At hour 14, the joint-settlement LMP is
23.77 EUR/MWh at N8 and 84.58 EUR/MWh at N2, a 60.81 EUR/MWh spread.

The important caveat is dual-price ambiguity. Settled profits for merchant
investors I1 and I2 are -38 and -299 EUR/day, while their last optimistic MPEC
profits are 13,476 and 5,659 EUR/day. Maximum MPEC-versus-joint LMP differences
are about 44.57 EUR/MWh. Treat this as an optimistic candidate equilibrium,
not a unique-price equilibrium.

Outputs are in
`model/output/epec/seidel_ieee9_congestion_100_seidel/` and are currently
untracked. The older handoff figures of 270.05 MW / 1290.57 MWh are stale or
inconsistent; the committed planner output for the current dataset is
238.924 MW / 826.862 MWh.
