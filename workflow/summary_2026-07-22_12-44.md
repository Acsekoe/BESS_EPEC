# Handoff Summary — Standalone Nodal-Access Auction Primal and Dual

Date: 2026-07-22 12:44 (Europe/Vienna)

## Current objective

Develop a strategic pay-as-bid nodal grid-access auction for BESS investment.
The intended Gauss–Seidel structure is:

1. I1 solves one best-response problem and submits one continuous
   `(quantity_mw, price_eur_per_mw_day)` pair at every node.
2. The auction clears and accepted MW enter the spot market.
3. I2 solves once against I1's updated bids, followed by I3 and I4.
4. Repeat outer iterations until bids, awards, MW, and MWh stabilize or cycle.

The desired final formulation must not use an outer grid or repeated scalar
search over power quantities. All nine bid quantities should be continuous
variables inside each investor's single best-response optimization.

## Agreed auction specification

- One bid block per investor and node: requested MW and EUR/MW/day.
- All IEEE-9 nodes are open for bidding.
- The auctioneer maximizes accepted bid value:

  `sum(i,n) bid_price[i,n] * award[i,n]`.

- Awards satisfy `0 <= award[i,n] <= bid_quantity[i,n]` and
  `sum(i) award[i,n] <= node_limit[n]`.
- Awarded MW become installed BESS power and incur power CAPEX.
- Investors choose energy continuously within
  `2 * awarded_mw <= energy_mwh <= 8 * awarded_mw` and incur energy CAPEX.
- Pay-as-bid access payment is `bid_price * awarded_mw` and is a transfer in
  social-cost accounting.
- Exact equal-price marginal bids are currently allowed to remain non-unique in
  the LP. Do not add nonlinear pro-rata constraints unless equal bids actually
  occur and materially affect a strategic solve.

## Equal-price caveat

The primal LP has multiple optimal allocations if equal-price bids are marginal
and their combined requested quantity exceeds remaining capacity. Ipopt may
select any optimal split. This is currently accepted as a provisional convention.

Exact quantity-proportional rationing would require a secondary allocation rule.
With strategic bid quantities, proportional equations or objectives introduce
bilinear/nonlinear terms. The decision is therefore to leave the LP linear and
only revisit the tie rule if equal bids occur in the strategic solution.

## Discarded computational approach

An experimental driver placed continuous requested MW in an outer SciPy scalar
search. Every trial quantity cleared the auction and solved a full investor MPEC.
A four-investor, all-node first iteration required:

- I1: 81 MPEC solves;
- I2: 82 MPEC solves;
- I3: 105 MPEC solves;
- I4: 114 MPEC solves;
- total: 382 MPEC solves and about 24 minutes;
- two I1 solves failed, so the iteration was not valid.

That driver and its output were removed. Do not recreate an outer quantity grid
or scalar-search architecture. The quantity itself is not the problem; it must
be an endogenous variable inside one investor best-response model.

## Planned strategic integration

For each active investor, hold rival bids fixed and embed both auction and spot
market optimality in one best-response problem:

1. Active bid quantities and prices are upper-level variables for all nodes.
2. Auction awards are primal variables.
3. Auction primal feasibility, dual feasibility, and strong duality enforce
   bid-value-maximizing allocation.
4. Link active installed power exactly to its auction award.
5. Pass all investors' awards into spot-market storage capacities.
6. Optimize active energy capacity, dispatch, and market profit.
7. Subtract `bid_price * award` from active profit.
8. Update that investor's bid and award once, then move to the next investor in
   Gauss–Seidel order.

This introduces smooth bilinear terms but should require one combined strategic
solve per investor, rather than hundreds of fixed-quantity MPEC evaluations.
