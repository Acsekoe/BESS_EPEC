# Handoff: return to the regular spot-market EPEC

## Current objective

Resume the four-investor spot-market EPEC in `model/epec_diagonalization.py` and
test whether its investment result is robust to starting values and update
choices. The auction branch is no longer the main thesis path.

## Decision on the auction branch

The two-follower access-auction MPEC should not be developed further for the
main analysis. Its first Gauss-Seidel sweep exposed two additional problems on
top of the existing electricity-price ambiguity:

- IPOPT accepted a local I2 response with about -2.55 kEUR/day even though the
  merchant investor can stay out and earn approximately zero;
- strategic bids landed exactly on rival prices, so the optimistic embedded
  auction and independent reclearing differed by more than 44 MW at a node.

A defensible auction would require an explicit deterministic tie rule, further
best-response safeguards, and substantially more sensitivity analysis. That is
a separate mechanism-design extension, not necessary for the current research
question. The experiment, bid profiles, code, and diagnostic outputs are
archived under `model/auction/`.

## Maintained model and known caveat

Use the regular one-follower formulation:

- `model/single_investor_mpec.py`: one investor chooses BESS MW/MWh and embeds
  the spot-market optimality conditions;
- `model/epec_diagonalization.py`: four-investor Jacobi/Seidel diagonalization
  with shared nodal headroom, damping, projection, and checkpointing.

This path previously converged near 239 MW / 827 MWh, close to the planner, but
still uses optimistic lower-level dual prices. Initialization tests do not cure
that dual-price ambiguity. Continue to report optimistic MPEC and independent
joint-settlement profits separately and retain the planned dual-face analysis.

## Proposed first diagnostic

Run exactly one undamped Gauss-Jacobi sweep from the common 10 MW / 2-hour seed.
All investors then respond to the same initial state, giving a clean diagnostic
of their simultaneous local desired investments before iterative feedback:

```powershell
python model\epec_diagonalization.py `
  --data model\data\processed\market_data_IEEE_9Bus_congestion.json `
  --investor-set portfolio4 `
  --update-rule jacobi `
  --damping 1.0 `
  --node-limit-mw 100 `
  --seed-power-mw 10 `
  --seed-ratio-hours 2 `
  --max-iters 1 `
  --max-cpu-time 120 `
  --output-dir model\output\epec\diagnostic_jacobi_seed10
```

This sweep is not an equilibrium. Inspect each investor's solver termination,
proposed nodal MW/MWh, optimistic objective, strong-duality gap, shared-limit
violations, and any projection events. The projected post-sweep state must not
be interpreted as the investors' unconstrained desired response; use the
exported proposed capacities for that comparison.

## Sensitivity sequence after the diagnostic

Run damped Gauss-Seidel (`damping=0.7`, `max-iters=20`) from at least:

1. low start: 1 MW per investor-node, 2-hour ratio;
2. baseline start: 10 MW, 2-hour ratio;
3. dense start: 25 MW, 4-hour ratio.

Then add an investor-order sensitivity, preferably the reverse order
I4-I3-I2-I1. The current CLI fixes the portfolio order, so an explicit
`--investor-order` option should be added before that test rather than manually
rewriting the investor definitions.

Compare across runs:

- convergence status and failed solves;
- aggregate and nodal MW/MWh;
- ownership shares and E/P ratios;
- projection events and binding nodal limits;
- optimistic MPEC versus joint-settlement profits;
- sensitivity of the result to seed and solve order.

Treat agreement in aggregate capacity with small relocation among N3/N9 as
robust aggregate behavior but weakly identified siting. Materially different
totals, persistent projection, cycling, or order-dependent ownership require
further diagnosis before calling the result an equilibrium candidate.
